Game modes

ModeDescription
ClassicThe game as you know it, 3 revealed starters, 10 Gym Badges available, need 8 to advance
ProWild encounters and starters stay covered (blind pick) until you click one to reveal it
NuzlockeSame blind picks as Pro, plus permadeath (a fainted Pokemon is gone for good), no Revives, no Casino access, and pricier PokeStop restocks

Each mode has its own separate ranking.

Battle status effects

StatusEffect
PoisonLoses a fixed percentage of max HP at the end of each turn, until cured or the battle ends
BurnLoses a fixed percentage of max HP at the end of each turn and deals reduced physical move damage; Fire-type Pokemon are immune
SleepCan't act for a random number of turns, waking up automatically afterward

Battle item limits

During a single battle, you can use at most:

Potion2 per battle
Revive1 per battle

The limit resets every new battle, regardless of how many you're carrying in your inventory.

Multi-path evolution

Some Pokemon have more than one possible evolution path (e.g. Eevee, Poliwhirl, Kirlia). In those cases, the game randomly rolls which path your Pokemon takes, with equal odds between every option.

Exception, Mega Evolution: Pokemon with two different Mega Evolutions (Charizard, Mewtwo, Raichu, among others) let you manually choose which one to use at the moment of Mega Evolving.

Cruise Casino (Fishing and Lucky Spin)

During Cruise Ship stops, a screen offers two mini-events, each usable only once for the whole run (not per stop):

Fishing: cast a line once for a shot at a water-dwelling Pokemon.

Lucky Spin: spin a prize wheel once. Not available in Nuzlocke mode.

Automatic save

The game automatically saves your progress during a run. If you close your browser mid-run, you can pick up where you left off on the same device when you come back, the save stays available for up to 7 days.
